Data tool for analyzing health conditions based on demographics

Data from U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance




Preliminary observations:

  • The correlation between a state's median income and the share of smokers, obese, and those lacking healthcare is generally negative
  • Roughly, as age increases, the share of people lacking healthcare decreases, less so the rate of obese; no correlation with smoking
  • Finally, its is generally the case that the higher the poverty rate, the higher the rates of obesity, and of those lacking healthcare or smoking
